What is Sustainable Urban Mobility?

Sustainable urban mobility focuses on developing transport systems that enable free, safe, and efficient movement in urban areas with minimal environmental impact. It prioritizes public transit, non-motorized transport, and clean technologies to promote healthier cities and reduce fossil fuel reliance.

Review of Sustainable Urban Mobility Indicators Internationally

Globally, sustainable urban mobility indicators include air quality, modal split, accessibility, and traffic congestion. Monitoring these indicators helps cities track progress, make informed decisions, and meet international goals like the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs). Examples from around the world highlight how policies can improve urban mobility and reduce environmental effects.

Importance and Challenges of Visualizing Mobility Data

Visualizing mobility data is key to understanding traffic patterns, evaluating transport policies, and informing policymakers. Challenges include integrating diverse data sources, ensuring accuracy, and presenting data in an accessible way to support sustainable transport strategies.
